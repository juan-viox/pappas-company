About Pappas

A 33-year Watertown institution.

George and Mike Pappas founded the company in 1992 with a simple idea: the dealer who sells a movable wall should also be the dealer who walks the job site, writes the acoustic detail, and commissions the final install. Three decades later, the shop at 12 Bridge Street is still family-run.

Every installation goes out with a W-2 Pappas crew. No subcontracted labor. No handoffs between trades. Our estimators are factory-certified on every line we sell — Modernfold, Skyfold, Smoke Guard, Renlita, Draper — and we've specified into the buildings architects and GCs in Boston, Cambridge, Providence, and Hartford point to when they want to show their best work.

How We Work

Four stages, one team.

Specification — we sit with architects on day one.
01 — Specification

We sit with architects on day one.

BIM families, CSI three-part specs, acoustic and life-safety details. We speak the drawing, not the product sheet. From the first schematic review, we're the voice in the room who knows what Modernfold can and can't do.

02 — Design Consult

Our estimators walk the job site.

STC ratings, fire ratings, sightlines, clearances, ceiling cavities, seismic bracing. Every pre-install walkthrough is led by a senior estimator with 500+ sites behind them — not a rep reading off a cut sheet.

03 — Factory-Certified Install

W-2 Pappas crews. No subcontractors.

Every line we sell is installed by a crew factory-certified for that product. Modernfold, Skyfold, Smoke Guard, Renlita. We commission to spec. We field-test acoustic performance. We deliver a wall that meets the rating on the drawing.

04 — Lifetime Service

24-hour emergency response, six states.

Operable walls are mechanical systems — they need preventive maintenance. Life-safety products need it faster. We hold service contracts across New England with 24-hour response, and every job we install stays on our books for the life of the building.
